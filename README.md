## Skills

#### Problem Solving
My previous roles in transport engineering were mainly based on solving problems through design, planning and analytical thinking. In particular, building a transport simulation model (including highway network coding) required a similar process to debugging to achieve a robust model. Once I got errors from the model run, my approach would be to break it down into smaller parts, analyse its key factors, do the appropriate research, and then fix the errors. If errors still existed, I repeated all these steps. Using this approach, I was able to assist the team effectively on various projects.

#### Communication and Collaboration 
My previous roles have involved a significant amount of relationship management, which I took pride in carrying out successfully. During my time working for private engineering consultancies, my role involved close liaison with other internal departments as well as establishing rapport with both private and public sector clients. Whilst working for a local authority, building strong relationships with key stakeholders and funding bodies was essential to my role. These skills were beneficial whilst undertaking group engineering projects at Makers Academy, as I played a big part in motivating and supporting our team to keep going.

#### Adaptability
At the beginning of my most recent contract for the HS2 project, I was required to analyse previous compliance documents, over 3,000 pages, to carry out my work and provide advice to other multidisciplinary teams. The task was made more challenging as I had to follow completely new procedures due to HS2 being a high profile project. I remained flexible and proactive in managing my tasks through self-learning, asking pertinent questions of my team, and attending extra meetings with other teams. My adaptability and willingness to learn in this new role meant that I received positive feedback from the team.
